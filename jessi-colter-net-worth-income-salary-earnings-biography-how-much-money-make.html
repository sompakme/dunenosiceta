Jessi Colter (born Miriam Johnson on May 25, 1943) is an American country music singer best known for her 1975 country-pop crossover hit “I’m Not Lisa” with her husband, country singer and composer Waylon Jennings. Jessi Colter was one of the few female artists to emerge from the “outlaw” movement of the mid-to-late 1970s. Colter followed a career in country music after meeting her future husband, releasing her debut studio album, A Country Star Is Born, in 1970. Colter signed with Capitol Records five years later and recorded her debut solo song, “I’m Not Lisa,” which went to No. 1 on the country charts and No. 5 on the mainstream charts. She was included on the collaborative LP Wanted: The Outlaws in 1976, which went platinum and helped her establish herself as one of the few female outlaw country singers.
