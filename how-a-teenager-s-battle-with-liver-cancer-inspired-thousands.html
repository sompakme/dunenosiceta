His death has left a deep void in the hearts of his family, friends, schoolmates, and community, who remember him as a bright, kind, and brave young man.

AJ Fetty’s Journey: From Mono to Cancer

Fetty’s ordeal began in September 2023, when he went to the hospital with symptoms of mononucleosis, a common viral infection that causes fever, sore throat, and fatigue. However, further tests revealed that he had multiple tumors in his liver, which were later diagnosed as hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), the most common type of liver cancer.

HCC is a rare disease in children and adolescents, accounting for less than 1% of all pediatric cancers. The causes of HCC in young patients are not well understood, but some risk factors include chronic liver diseases, genetic disorders, and exposure to toxins. The prognosis of HCC depends on the stage of the disease, the size and number of tumors, the liver function, and the response to treatment.

AJ’s condition was very serious, as he had advanced tumors that caused severe complications, such as bleeding, aneurysm, and infection. He was transferred to the Children’s Hospital of Pittsburgh, where he received intensive care and underwent several procedures to stabilize his condition and manage his symptoms. He also celebrated his 16th birthday at the hospital on October 10, surrounded by his loved ones.

AJ Fetty’s doctors hoped to start him on cancer medication once his liver had healed enough from the previous interventions. They also considered the possibility of a liver transplant if the tumors shrank enough to make him eligible for surgery. However, AJ’s condition deteriorated rapidly in the last week of October, when more tumors eroded his blood vessels and caused massive bleeding. He received blood transfusions and another procedure to patch the bleed, but his body could not cope with the stress. He passed away peacefully on October 30, with his family by his side.
