You, Dry Needling, and the Great State of Washington: A Quest for Pinpoint Relief (Maybe with a Side of Laughter)

Ah, dry needling. Those tiny needles wiggling their way into your tight muscles, supposedly banishing pain like a podiatrist chasing away rogue socks. But can chiropractors in Washington state join the fun needle party? Buckle up, because this is where things get interesting...and maybe a touch confusing.

Hold on, what's dry needling anyway?

Imagine a muscle so tense it's basically become a grumpy little gremlin. Dry needling aims to deactivate those gremlins by sticking a thin needle into those tight spots, supposedly relaxing the muscle and easing your pain. Think of it as an acupuncture cousin who skipped the whole "chi flow" lecture.

So, can chiropractors in Washington get their needle on?

This is where the plot thickens faster than a bowl of undercooked oatmeal. As of May 2023, physical therapists in Washington got the green light to perform dry needling. But chiropractors? The jury's still out, or rather, the regulatory board is still pondering its pointy-needle policy.

Wait, what? No needle party for chiropractors?

Some chiropractic clinics in Washington say they offer dry needling, but it's a bit of a gray area. The official word from the Washington State Department of Health is a bit of a maybe-not-maybe-so situation.

Here's the not-so-funny part:

It's always best to be safe than sorry. If you're set on dry needling from a chiropractor, call your state's chiropractic licensing board to see if the practice is officially allowed.

But hey, there's good news too!

Physical therapists can now perform dry needling in Washington. So, if you're open to exploring that option, you've got some licensed needle ninjas at your disposal.

Alright, alright, I get it. Give me the bottom line.

Here's the short and shifty:

  • Physical therapists in Washington: Yes to dry needling (as of May 2023)
  • Chiropractors in Washington: The answer is a bit of a ¯_(ツ)_/¯ for now. Check with the licensing board for the latest info.

Bonus Round: Dry Needling FAQ

How to find a physical therapist who performs dry needling in Washington?

Ask your doctor for a referral or search online for clinics offering dry needling services.

How to prepare for a dry needling session?

Loose-fitting clothing is your friend. Let your therapist know about any medications you're taking and any concerns you might have.

How much does dry needling cost?

Costs can vary depending on your insurance and the clinic. Be sure to ask about pricing beforehand.

How many dry needling sessions will I need?

It depends on your individual situation. Your therapist will discuss a treatment plan with you.

How long does dry needling last?

A typical session can last anywhere from 15 to 30 minutes.
